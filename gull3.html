Physical Computing - Week 6 + 7 + 8

Physical Computing Identification.


First attempt at making an LED light up.

Understanding the organisation of the breadboard was hardest here, as I had never interacted with one before. I think this task would be much easier for me to complete now, having made more complex interactive pieces.


Motion Sensor.

This is a motion sensor that beeps when an object approaches it within a certain distance, and was later placed onto a paper bag to make a sort of "safety helmet" that would buzz when the user would get too close to something in their path.

The challenge here was getting the circuit and breadboard to stay on top of the paper bag. The tech kept falling off and was far too heavy. After some trial and error and copious amounts of tape, it eventually stuck.

This gave me a good insight into how wearable tech works in reality, which would later inform my ideation and plan for my final project, which is essentially a synth helmet.



Potato Synth - Intersecting touch and sound.

This is no ordinary potato - this is a synth potato. When touched, it emits a sound. After some inspection of the code, I was able to alter the pitch of the potato's sound, and attach multiple different wires to create a working synth.
