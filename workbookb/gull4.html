Irony Machine

The irony machine is a creative project created by an anonymous Argentine creative coder. The site is designed to be lost in - with each different “room” you click on taking you down a different rabbit hole. The UI is built like a nostalgic pixelated video game, and is like waking through a time machine of early internet design. I guess there is no real purpose for the Irony Machine, other than to kill time and have fun.


Anna's Secret Garden

Anna’s Secret Garden lets you draw your own flower and add it to the cyber garden! You can also browse the flower gallery, and see what others have “planted” in the garden.


Petra Cotright

Petra Cotright is an American artist, who works in both analogue and digital mediums. Her website is a tedious and confusing design, that showcases her artwork in an unexpected way. Her website feels like a step back in time to the basic designs and aesthetics of 200s internet sites. Even though I have to scroll all the way down to a neverending homepage to view her works, I think its worth it.

EyeWriter - Zach Lieberman

In 2003, the graffiti artist ,TEMPT1, was diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), which resulted in his paralysis. In response, Zach Lieberman - an American Computer programmer, designer and new media artist - alongside a team of computer programmers and developers created the EyeWriter. The EyeWriter is a device that allowed TEMPT1 to continue drawing through eye-tracking technology and software. The device captures the eye-movements of TEMPT1 and directs them from a laptop by his bedside to a remote laser-tagging device, which {graffitis} his artwork onto buildings around his city via projection.


West Ham - Sculpture for Football Songs

"Sculpture for Football Songs" is an installation by Angela Bulloch, first exhibited in 1998. Belisha beacons are linked to a microphone in the gallery and initiate a sequence of flashing lights when responding to sound. The colours of the lights reflect the colours of the West Ham football strip.
